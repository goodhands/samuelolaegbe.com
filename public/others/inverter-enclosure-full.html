Load path & anchoring

120kg battery transfers 100% through the welded steel plinth to the floor slab — wall carries zero static weight
Anti-tip only: 4x M10 threaded rods in hollow-brick chemical anchors (2 at top bay, 2 at mid-shelf) — resist tipping, not deadweight
No expansion bolts — hollow brick webs would shatter. Chemical resin + mesh sleeve only
Thermal split: insulated mid-shelf stops inverter exhaust heat reaching the battery
Z-baffle vents on side panels — air turns twice before reaching either bay, blocking driving rain

Don't cut: plinth steel spec, chemical anchor system, or the battery door gasket — these aren't cost padding, they're the two things (structural safety and zero-water-tolerance sealing) the whole design exists to solve.

Stealth camera recommendation

Best option for this build: rather than buying a disguised "spy camera" product, embed a small covert camera module (just the lens + board, no visible housing) inside the roof overhang or one of the Z-baffle vent hoods you're already having welded. It's genuinely more discreet than anything off-the-shelf, because it's not a separate object someone could spot or steal — it's built into the structure. Wire it off the same 12V line as the fan/thermostat, on its own fuse.

Reliable brands to build around: Reolink (Argus/Lumus mini series) or EZVIZ mini outdoor modules — both are legitimately weatherproof (IP65+), have solid low-light performance, and are sold through mainstream Nigerian retailers rather than only sketchy marketplace listings. Avoid generic "disguised as X" spy-cam products for this — most are indoor-rated, battery life is often under 2 hours, and long-term reliability outdoors is inconsistent. For something protecting a ~₦2m+ system, a known brand's mini module is worth the extra cost.

Placement: tuck it under the roof's forward overhang facing down/out — it stays dry, sits in shadow (harder to spot from below), and covers exactly the approach angle someone would use to reach the doors.
